Say hello, new design


Merina Leong and Andrea Saksono worked closely in collaboration on the project with the latest technology, usability and extensibility in mind. The redesign is the first significant change to COMP 1950 course since it launched in 2011. Over the next few months, you’ll see other components around COMP 1950 aligning around this new direction.

Andrea, our lead designer and user experience researcher, main goal was to focus on discovering and understanding students’ problem and build a human-centered solution. She translated insight into multiple solutions to address those needs, then conducting A/B testing to validate our solution. From our sitemap, to navigation, to information architecture, Andrea took a data-intensive approach to ensure we had the best solution moving forward. Additionally, she build out our proposal meta site.

Merina, our frontend developer, main goal was to focus on modernizing the COMP 1950 tech stack and developing the prototype site. Additionally, as Project Manager, she implemented a Kanban process and slicing tasks that could be worked on in parallel by the team. To ensure the team worked cohesively, git source control was standardized amongst the team along with daily check ins via our slack workspace.

See the rationale for more information about our tech stack, design and usability improvements.
